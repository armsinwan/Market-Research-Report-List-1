<p><p>The Passive Infra-Red (PIR) occupancy sensors market is primarily divided into ceiling mount and wall mount sensors. Ceiling mount occupancy sensors are typically installed on ceilings to detect motion in large areas, effectively monitoring occupancy in spaces like offices and warehouses. In contrast, wall mount occupancy sensors are positioned on walls, making them ideal for smaller rooms or corridors. Both types utilize infrared technology to sense changes in heat patterns, enhancing energy efficiency by ensuring lights are activated only when spaces are occupied.</p></p>
