<p><p>Radio Frequency (RF) relays are crucial components in communication and electronic systems, categorized into DC and AC types based on their operating voltage. DC type RF relays operate on direct current, primarily used in low-voltage applications, offering fast switching times and reliability. In contrast, AC type RF relays work with alternating current, suitable for higher voltage applications. Both types serve essential roles in signal switching, enhancing the efficiency of RF systems in various industries, including telecommunications and broadcasting.</p></p>
